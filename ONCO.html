Oncology

Oncology is a branch of medicine that specialises in the diagnosis and treatment of cancer. It includes. Medical oncology (the use of chemotherapy, immunotherapy, hormone therapy, and other drugs to treat cancer), Radiation oncology (the use of radiation therapy to treat cancer) Oncology is an user-friendly, evidence-based application for oncologists to prescribe treatment plans for cancer patients based on symptoms, cancer stage, and other clinical parameters. This application will help the medical staff to best cater their patients and maintain their register without overhead of written paper work. It will also help to access patient’s previous history, thereby bringing more accuracy in the treatment. Oncology will be developed and rolled out in multiple phases enhancing the use for doctors/health workers and later by the patients.

Problem Statement

Oncologists and medical staff often face significant challenges in managing cancer treatment plans due to:
  • Reliance on Manual Processes: Paperwork and manual entry increase the likelihood of errors and delays.
  • Treatment Planning Challenges: Lack of a unified, evidence-based platform hinders the ability to prescribe appropriate treatments quickly and accurately.
  • Collaboration Issues: Lack of real-time communication between oncologists and support staff results in a breakdown in treatment monitoring and care coordination.
  • Data Entry Burden: Medical support staff spend considerable time manually entering and verifying data, leading to delays and possible errors in patient records.

Target Audience

  • Oncologists and Medical Practitioners: Primary users who diagnose cancer and prescribe treatment plans. They will use the application to streamline the prescription of treatments like chemotherapy, immunotherapy, and radiation therapy based on patient symptoms, cancer stage, and clinical parameters.
  • Medical Support Staff (Nurses, Administrative Staff): Users who assist oncologists and manage patient records, scheduling, and other administrative tasks.

Goal & Objectives

The goal of the platform is to:
  • Streamline Cancer Treatment Planning: To provide oncologists with a user-friendly, digital platform that simplifies the process of patient registration, prescribing and managing cancer treatment plans based on patient symptoms, cancer stage, and other clinical parameters.
  • Enhance Accuracy, Efficiency and User Experience: To improve the accuracy of treatment decisions and reduce the risk of errors by offering an evidence-based tool that integrates patient history and clinical data in real-time.
  • Reduce Administrative Burden: To eliminate the reliance on paper-based record-keeping, thereby reducing the overhead of manual paperwork and freeing up time for healthcare professionals to focus on patient care.
  • Facilitate Access to Patient Data: To ensure that medical staff have easy, secure access to a patient’s medical history, previous treatments, and clinical parameters, improving the continuity and quality of care.
  • Updates and Notifications To allow patients to set reminders for checkups, appointments and medication dosage medication tracking.

Thought Process & Approcach

A user-friendly Oncology Treatment Management Platform that streamlines the process of prescribing, tracking, and managing cancer treatment plans. The platform integrates clinical evidence-based decision support and provides comprehensive access to patient data in a digital format, improving collaboration between oncologists and medical support staff. Key Features:
  • Unified Patient Dashboard: Displays comprehensive patient information, including treatment history, cancer stage, ongoing treatments, and key clinical parameters, in one place. Easy-to-navigate interface with customizable views for different users (oncologists, nurses, admin staff).
  • Evidence-Based Treatment Suggestions: Provides oncologists with treatment recommendations based on the latest clinical research and patient-specific data (e.g., symptoms, cancer stage, treatment history). Auto-suggests treatments based on entered data, reducing time spent searching for options.
  • Automated Treatment Prescription Tool: Oncologists can prescribe treatment directly from the platform by selecting the desired treatment modality (chemotherapy, immunotherapy, etc.) based on patient parameters. Pre-built templates for different cancer stages reduce the time spent on customization.
  • Real-Time Collaboration: Medical staff can access the same patient dashboard and instantly see any updates made by oncologists. Alerts and notifications are sent to relevant staff when patient data is updated or when treatments are modified. Integrated chat and messaging systems to foster communication between departments.
  • Progress Tracking: Nurses can update patient responses to treatment and any side effects through a streamlined interface. Visual charts of treatment progress allow oncologists to monitor how patients are responding over time, enabling quicker adjustments to treatment plans.
  • Automated Data Entry & Record Keeping: Integration with external medical systems to automatically pull in lab results, diagnostic imaging, and other relevant data. Automates patient record updates and reduces the burden of manual data entry.

Information Architecture

The IA for an intuitive, streamlined experience for oncologists and support staff. This structure will ensure easy access to treatment protocols, patient records, and communication tools.
Dashboard (Home Page)
  • Overview of patient treatment schedules, critical updates, and recent activity.
  • Quick access to active patient cases, alerts, and pending tasks.
Patient Management
  • View/search for patients with filter options (e.g., name, ID, stage of treatment).
  • Form to register a new patient, including key medical history and personal details.
  • Detailed view of each patient's history, diagnosis, and past treatments.
  • Repository of evidence-based treatment guidelines for cancer types, stages, and available therapies (chemotherapy, immunotherapy, etc.).
  • Form to prescribe a customized treatment plan for each patient, including dose, duration, and frequency.
  • Comprehensive, chronological record of each patient’s treatment history.
  • Repository for lab results, imaging, pathology reports, and other relevant documents.
  • Calendar integration for scheduling patient appointments, follow-ups, and reviews.
  • Notifications for upcoming treatments, necessary follow-ups, and any urgent actions.

UX Flow

The UX Flow, detailing the sequence of steps a user would follow to accomplish key tasks within the system, including patient registration, treatment planning, and record management.
Flow 1: Patient Registration and Profile Setup
  • The oncologist opens a portal and starts creating a patients profile by selecting “New Patient from the Patient Management section.
  • A form appears with fields for patient details: name, contact, demographics, diagnosis, and medical history.
  • After patient details are entered, the system prompts the user to enter the initial diagnosis, stage, and treatment history (if available).
  • Upon entering all required information, the user confirms the profile setup, and the patient’s details are saved.
  • The patient’s record is now accessible under “Patient List” for easy future reference.
Flow 2: Creating a New Treatment Plan
  • The oncologist opens a patient profile and selects “Create Treatment Plan.”
  • The system prompts for treatment type selection (e.g., chemotherapy, immunotherapy).
  • The system provides suggested treatment protocols based on cancer type and stage.
  • The oncologist customizes the plan (e.g., frequency, dosage, and start/end dates).
  • The system performs an automated compliance check to ensure protocol guidelines are met.
  • If issues are detected (e.g., dosage exceeding limits), the system flags the treatment plan and provides suggestions for adjustments.
  • The oncologist reviews the plan summary and confirms details.
  • Upon confirmation, the plan is saved and scheduled reminders are set for treatment milestones.
Flow 3: Monitoring Patient Progress
  • The oncologist accesses the Dashboard to see a list of active patients and their treatment progress.
  • Color-coded statuses indicate which patients are on schedule, need follow-up, or have upcoming treatments.
  • The oncologist selects a patient from the dashboard and goes to their profile.
  • Under “Notes & Observations,” the oncologist adds recent lab results, side effects, or other clinical observations.
  • If an observation indicates a potential issue (e.g., adverse reaction), the system notifies the oncologist and relevant support staff for immediate action.
Flow 4: Collaboration and Real-Time Communication with Record Management
  • The system allows tagging specific staff members for tasks such as follow-up appointments or additional lab tests.
  • Notifications appear instantly, enabling staff to respond or add necessary notes directly to the patient’s profile.
  • The system displays a chronological view of treatment milestones, lab results, and observations.
  • Records can be exported for use in other systems or printed for offline reference.
